About the Project

This project visualises data from the World Temperature dataset repository from Kaggle. Our dataset offers an extensive view of temperature trends, capturing time-series values in degrees Celsius for the 1000 most populous cities worldwide, spanning from January 1, 1980, to September 30, 2020. Comprising 24 columns, the data encompasses a broad spectrum of information related to city temperatures, trends, and variations. Through in-depth analysis, we have successfully uncovered significant patterns and insights within this vast dataset. These findings are poised to inform strategic decisions and enhance understanding of global climate trends, potentially influencing both local and international environmental strategies. Through these visualisations, we aim to:

  • Show the change in temperature of cities in the United Kingdom over the years grouped by region using Interactive Multiple Line Chart.
  • Study the relationship between population and average decade temperature scores over time for Cities in the United Kingdom (1980-2020) using Interactive Scatter Plot.
  • Study the temperature trends and compare across seven cities in the United Kingdom over time for top and bottom-ranked countries using Interactive Stacked Bar Chart.
  • Explore the minimum, maximum and average temperature scores from 2010-2019 (a decade) across the world Using Interactive World Map.
